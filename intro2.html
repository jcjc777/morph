A Brief Introduction

3D Modeling

Since 3D designing covers a few categories, the focus of this site is on 3D modeling and printing; the creation of an object which has 3 dimensions (height, width, depth) in a virtual environment with the goal of having it printed (as opposed to 3D Rendering.) While the technology had been around for more than 30 years, it had only been used for broad applications within the past decade. It's advancement as a big consumer technology had somehow stagnated but it is evolving to include a wide range of professional enterprise applications across various industries, like aerospace, automotive, medical, retail, military/defense, and other professional services. The growth of industrial applications for 3D modeling and printing are projected to be about 18% per year over the next 5 years.

3D Modeling Softwares

The list of softwares for 3D modeling and printing are long and varied. You can check out a curated list via the above title. Each one offers their pros and cons and suited for different experience level, product categories and industries. In order to have a better idea of the functions and user friendliness of each, one can check out the countless demonstrative videos at Youtube.

Some are offered based on a subscription with an annual or a monthly charge while a few are free to students of an affiliated learning institution. The list also include those that are free with a registered account, namely Tinkercad, a popular intuitive easy-to-use 3D CAD design tool.

As a progression, one can move on to Fusion 360, a cloud based 3D CAD, CAM and CAE software. It is especially popular with product developers, engineers and hobbyists. Then there is Meshmixer,a free 3D sculpting-based CAD (Computer Assisted Design) program created by Autodesk which is popular in designing toys, sculptures and jewelries.

3D Printing

From the succesfully finished 3D designs stored in files, we move on to the printing process so that our hard work and creativities are transformed into physical objects.

In order to attain professional or manufactured quality prints, there are a whole hosts of online 3D printing services and companies. Most of these services have more than one physical printing locations and a presence in the New York metro area. These services vary in the printing material they specialized in, prices and printing requirements. The notable ones that are popular and higly rated among porfessioanls from various industries are, Makelab, Shapeways, Kwambio and PrintAWorld.

The materials used in 3D printing are semmingly endless with new process and materials added with the growth of the industry. Some more common materials currently used are ABS plastic, PLA, polyamide (nylon), glass filled polyamide, stereolithography materials (epoxy resins), silver, titanium, steel, wax, photopolymers, polycarbonate porcelain and sand stone.

3D Printers

A 3D printer is a machine allowing the creation of a physical object from a three-dimensional digital model, typically by laying down many thin layers of a material in succession. While the technology has been around for a few decades, the development had not reach mass market until about 10 years ago. Since then with many news stories, advertisements and promotions, 3D printers and printing had penetrated our common lexicon.

The range of 3D printers varies based on the processes, and working materials that differ in characteristics. They are further divided into types and technologies involved; and then the consumer or professional enterprise application marker segments.

The recent 3D design growth and broader professional enterprise applications had prompted big players like GE and HP to make rapid foray into the industry. The brands that covers both market segments include Stratasys, Makerbot, Ultimaker and many more.
