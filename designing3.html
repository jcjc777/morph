The Designing Process

The designing experience can stretch your imagination and creativity in a very rewarding and elating way. The designing process leading to succesful printable objects can be fun, frustrating, facinating; one after another or all at the same time. The only way to find out is to go for the experience and challenge yourself. Staying focus on the design is key to getting through the frustrative road blocks. You many end up surprising yourself.

While it may not be for all, the experience is very individualized. For a snapshot of the experience, check out the profiles of 3 recent graduates of a six week introductory course in 3D design and printing titled "Make it, Print it, Sell it" at the New York Public Library. DD Glow, Infinite Star and Scharfer Stürmer had recently participated in the graduation exhibition at the SIBL branch of the New York Public Library that showcased their succesful designs.

To the uninitiated, the following examples depict the process of two designs from sketch to print.

Boomerang Vase: From sketches to final printable design with Fusion 360.
Step 1

Create two conic curves (or other simpler shapes) sketches on 2 workplanes.

Step 2

Use the "Extrude" function to build the sketches into a 3 dimensional shape. Create an inset at face of the base (optional).

Step 3

With the "Shell" function, hollow out the shape to a thickness that is within the printable requirements.

Final Design

"Fillet" the edges all around so that all the thin edges are smoothed out to meet wall thickness requirements. Export the design in a STL format file for the print job.

Music Notes Ring: Sketches and SVG files to final design with Fusion 360.
Step 1

Cut the center part from the basic 3-dimensional ring created from circle sketches.

Step 2

Inset the "whole note" image designs imported in SVG format.

Step 3

Place and "Extrude" the single note image designs to create the central design element of the ring.

Final Design

"Fillet" away all sharp edges and make sure the thickness at all points of the design meets the printing requirements.
